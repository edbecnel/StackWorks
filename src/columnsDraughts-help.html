StackWorks — Columns Draughts Help & Rules

Columns Draughts combines International Draughts movement and capture rules on a 10×10 board with Lasca‑style stacking captures: captured pieces are not removed from the board but are instead stacked under the capturing piece, forming columns (stacks).

Rules Overview

Movement

  • Men: move diagonally forward one square.
  • Kings: "fly" on quiet moves — slide diagonally any distance in any direction until blocked (International Draughts flying king).

Captures (Mandatory + Multi‑capture + Max‑capture)

  • Mandatory: if any capture is available, you must capture.
  • Multi‑capture: after a capture you must continue capturing with the same moving stack until no further captures are available.
  • Max‑capture rule: if multiple capture lines exist, only those with the most total captures are legal (choose freely among ties).
  • Anti‑loop: during a capture chain you cannot jump the same square twice.

Stacking Capture (Lasca‑style)

  • When you jump a stack you capture only the top piece of that stack.
  • The captured piece is inserted at the bottom of the capturing stack.
  • The original stack loses its top piece but is otherwise undisturbed.

Promotion

  • Promotion happens at end‑of‑turn only.
  • If a Man reaches the far rank during a capture chain, it does not gain King powers mid‑chain. If it reaches the far rank at any point in the chain it promotes after the chain ends — even if it does not finish on the far rank.

Columns Draughts vs International Draughts

Columns Draughts uses exactly the same movement and capture rules as International Draughts (10×10, 20 pieces per side, flying Kings, backward Men captures, max‑capture priority). The only difference is what happens to a captured piece:

  • International Draughts: captured pieces are removed from the board after the sequence ends.
  • Columns Draughts: captured pieces are stacked — the top piece of the jumped stack is placed at the bottom of the capturing stack. Nothing is ever permanently removed.

This single change produces very different strategic dynamics: material count becomes less informative, and the game stays complex deep into the endgame because pieces can re‑emerge from stacks.

Columns Draughts vs Damasca International

Damasca International is the closest relative of Columns Draughts. Both use stacking captures on a built‑in checkerboard with flying Officers/Kings and max‑capture. The key differences are:

  • Board & starting pieces: Columns Draughts uses a 10×10 board with 20 pieces per side; Damasca International uses an 8×8 board with 12 per side.
  • King direction rule during a chain:
    • Damasca: Kings may not reverse direction 180° during a capture chain (no "backtracking" along the same diagonal).
    • Columns Draughts: Kings may capture in any direction at each step of a chain, including reversing — matching International Draughts king freedom.

In practice, the direction freedom for Kings in Columns Draughts allows longer capture chains and makes the max‑capture calculation more demanding for both players.

Game Setup

  • Board: 10×10
  • 20 pieces per side (Men)
  • Light (White) moves first
  • Pieces occupy the dark squares of the first four rows on each side (standard International Draughts starting layout).

Game Rules

Movement

  • Men: move diagonally forward one square to an empty dark square.
  • Kings: slide diagonally any distance in any direction to an empty dark square (flying king, no distance limit).

Promotion

  • A Man promotes to a King upon reaching the far rank.
  • Promotion happens at end of a capture sequence (no mid‑sequence King powers). If a Man reaches the far rank at any point during a capture chain, it promotes after the chain ends.

Winning

  • You win when the opponent has no legal moves (all their top pieces are blocked or all have been captured into stacks you control).

Dead‑play / draw end conditions (adjudicated)

  • Threefold repetition: if the exact same position (including full stack composition and order) occurs three times, the game ends immediately and is adjudicated.
  • No‑progress counter: if there is no capture, promotion, or Man advance for 40 plies, the game ends and is adjudicated.
  • King‑only fast path: if only Kings move (no Man moves at all) for 30 plies, the game ends and is adjudicated.
  • Current counters are shown in the left panel: Status → Dead‑play counters.

Captures & Multi‑Captures

Captures are mandatory when available. If you can continue capturing with the same stack after a capture, you must continue until no further captures are possible. When multiple capture lines exist, the maximum‑capture rule applies — you must choose a line with the greatest total number of captures.

How captures work

  • Men: capture by jumping diagonally over an adjacent enemy stack (forward or backward) to the empty square beyond it.
  • Kings: capture by sliding diagonally to an enemy stack, jumping over it, and landing on any empty square beyond it (flying capture). Multiple landing squares are available; the choice affects where the chain can continue next.
  • What is captured: only the top piece of the jumped stack. The captured piece is placed at the bottom of the capturing stack. The jumped stack loses its top but keeps any remaining pieces in place.

King direction freedom (vs Damasca)

During a capture chain, Kings in Columns Draughts can capture in any direction at each step — including reversing 180° along a diagonal. There is no direction restriction. This matches standard International Draughts king rules.

Anti‑loop rule

You cannot jump the same square twice in a single capture chain. This prevents infinite loops.

Stack Inspector

The Stack Inspector panel (right side) shows the full column of pieces under any square you hover or tap. Hover or click a square to inspect its stack from top (active, controls the piece) to bottom (buried).
  • The top piece of a stack is the one that moves and captures; pieces below it are "buried" but stay on the board.
  • A piece can escape from the bottom of a stack only if the pieces above it are gradually captured away over time.
  • Stack height is visible on each square in the board view (shown as a small number badge on tall stacks).

Using the Interface

Open Game Panel → Players to choose Human or Bot for Light and Dark. If you choose Bot, a second dropdown appears so you can select the bot level.

Online play (2 players)

Online play is available via the Start Page. Player 1 creates a room, then shares the Room ID with Player 2.

  • Room visibility: when creating a room, choose Public (spectators allowed) or Private (no public spectating).
  • Room ID is shown in‑game in Info → Online and can be copied with the button next to "Room ID".
  • Player 2 joins by pasting the Room ID on the Start Page.
  • Opponent presence: the Online panel shows opponent status with a small badge under the turn indicator (green=connected, yellow=in grace, red=disconnected, gray=waiting).
  • Spectating: you can spectate public rooms from the Start Page. Private rooms require a secret watch link.
  • Report issue: open Info → Online and click ⓘ to view/copy diagnostic info.

Board coordinates

  • Enable Board Coords in Options to show square labels on the board edge.
  • Enable Inside squares to move the labels inside each dark square.
  • Enable International square numbering to use the standard 1–50 numbering scheme used in International Draughts notation (forces inside‑square placement).

Board colors

Use Board & Pieces → Board to choose from Classic, Green, and Blue checkerboard color families.

Layout (Panels vs Menu)

StackWorks supports Panels mode (left/right of the board in landscape; top/bottom in portrait) and a small‑screen‑friendly Menu mode.

  • Change it via Options → Layout (Panels/Menu).
  • In Menu mode, tap ☰ to open panel sections as dialogs.

Keyboard shortcuts

Shortcuts use Ctrl (Windows/Linux) or Cmd (macOS). Shortcuts are disabled while typing in a text field.

  • Undo: Ctrl/Cmd+Z
  • Redo: Ctrl/Cmd+Y or Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+Z
  • Save: Ctrl/Cmd+S
  • Resign: Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+X
  • Full Screen: Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+F
  • Toggle layout: Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+L
  • Show keyboard shortcuts: Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+?
  • Click a piece to select it.
  • Legal destinations appear as highlighted circles.
  • Move History: Click any entry (including "Start") to jump to that point in the game. Undo/Redo also navigates through history.
  • During a capture chain you must continue with the same stack until the chain ends.
