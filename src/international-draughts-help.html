StackWorks — International Draughts Help & Rules

This page covers International Draughts on a 10x10 board as implemented in StackWorks.

Core identity: 20 men per side, flying kings, mandatory capture, continued capture chains, and maximum-capture priority.

Setup

  • Board: 10x10, using only the 50 dark squares
  • Pieces: 20 men per side
  • Starting rows: Black on 1-20, White on 31-50
  • First move: White

How Pieces Move

Men

  • Move one square diagonally forward on a non-capturing move.
  • Capture diagonally forward or backward.

Kings

  • Kings are flying kings.
  • They may move any distance diagonally over empty squares.
  • They capture from distance and may land on any empty square beyond the captured piece on the same diagonal.

Captures

  • Capturing is mandatory.
  • If a capture chain continues after the first jump, the same piece must keep capturing.
  • If multiple capture sequences exist, you must choose one that captures the greatest number of enemy pieces.
  • If several sequences tie for the maximum capture count, you may choose any of them.
  • During a capture sequence, the same enemy piece may not be captured twice.
  • During a capture sequence, a piece may pass through the same empty square more than once.
  • Captured pieces are removed only after the full capture sequence is finished.

Promotion timing: a man becomes a king only if it ends the move on the far promotion row. Passing through the promotion row during a capture chain does not grant king powers for that move.

Winning

  • You win by taking all of the opponent's pieces.
  • You also win if the opponent has the move but no legal move.

Draws

  • Repeated-position draws are supported through the shared StackWorks repetition flow.
  • The game is drawn after 25 successive moves by each player with only kings moving, no man move, and no capture.
  • The official reduced-material draw cases are also enforced:
    • 16 moves each: 3 kings vs 1 king
    • 16 moves each: 2 kings + 1 man vs 1 king
    • 16 moves each: 1 king + 2 men vs 1 king
    • 5 moves each: 2 kings vs 1 king
    • 5 moves each: 1 king + 1 man vs 1 king
    • 5 moves each: 1 king vs 1 king

StackWorks Notes

  • Side labels are shown as White and Black by default.
  • The status panel shows the 25-move king-only counter and any active reduced-material endgame timer.
  • Online and offline play both use the same International Draughts move, capture, promotion, and draw logic.
